Project Overview

A structured AI-driven system (AdvizMe.ai / “Callie”) designed to support and optimize automotive shop operations at Callahan Auto & Diesel. The system acts as an intelligent advisor for technicians and service advisors—analyzing inspections (DVIs), guiding estimates, prioritizing work, and improving communication, consistency, and profitability.

Core problem: fragmented workflows, inconsistent inspections, missed sales opportunities, poor documentation, and lack of systemized decision-making in a multi-location, high-volume shop environment.

Target users:

Service Advisors (Bob)
CEASR / Production Coordinator (Mitch)
Technicians (TC, Luis, others)
Owner/Diagnostic Lead (Preston)

Why it matters:

Direct impact on revenue, efficiency, and customer trust
Reduces reliance on tribal knowledge
Enables scalable, system-driven operations aligned with long-term goal of self-sustaining business
Core Objective

Build an AI-powered operational system that:

Standardizes inspections, estimates, and workflow decisions
Increases estimate accuracy, approval rates, and production flow
Acts as a real-time advisor/coach for staff
Reduces missed work, comebacks, and communication breakdowns
Allows the business to scale without relying on constant owner oversight

Priority Logic

Vehicles prioritized by:

Safety issues (brakes, leaks, steering, etc.)
Reliability risks (vehicle may fail soon)
Customer wait time / expectations
High-value jobs (revenue impact)
Stalled jobs (no progress)
Follow-ups / previously declined work

System should:

Flag “DO NOT DELAY” items
Surface hidden high-value opportunities
Prevent low-value work from blocking high-impact jobs
DVI / Inspection Standards

“Good” DVI includes:

Complete system coverage (no skipped sections)
Clear, specific notes (not vague)
Cause + effect + recommendation
High-quality photos:
Wide shot + close-up
Context + detail
Justification for every recommendation

Bad patterns:

“Needs brakes” (no detail)
No photos
No explanation of risk
Missing related systems (e.g., leak but no source investigation)
Estimate / Audit Standards

Good estimate:

Every DVI finding mapped to a line item
Includes diagnostic steps where needed
No missing labor for identified issues
Structured logically (diag → repair → parts)

System should flag:

Findings without estimate items
Estimates without supporting DVI evidence
Missing diagnostics
Underbuilt jobs
