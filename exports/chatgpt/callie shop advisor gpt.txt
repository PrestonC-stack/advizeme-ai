Project Overview

Internal advisor intelligence system (“Callie”) for Callahan Auto & Diesel designed to standardize service advisor decision-making, enforce inspection/estimate quality, and improve shop productivity, documentation, and customer communication. It acts as a structured thinking layer over existing workflows (TechMetric, DVI process), not a replacement for advisors.

Primary value:

Eliminates inconsistency in advisor performance
Prevents missed findings, under-scoped estimates, and liability exposure
Improves throughput, staging, and scheduling discipline
Converts raw DVI data into structured, actionable plans

Reference knowledge base:

Core Objective

Build an execution-ready advisor system that:

Forces completeness (300% rule)
Structures every repair decision using consistent frameworks (3Cs, P1–P4, confidence levels)
Converts inspections into staged, schedulable repair plans
Protects shop liability while improving customer clarity and approval rates

Priority Logic
P1: Active, safety-critical, or same-day expectation
P2: No update / missing DVI or verification
P3: Approved, in progress
P4: Waiting (authorization, parts, scheduling)

Priority must include:

WHY it is that level
WHAT moves it forward
