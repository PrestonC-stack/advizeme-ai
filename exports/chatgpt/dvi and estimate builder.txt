Project Overview

A structured Service Advisor + Technician workflow system for Callahan Auto & Diesel that converts fragmented shop data (DVIs, notes, scan results, photos, estimates) into organized, actionable intelligence. The system functions as an operational backbone and “execution layer” to standardize inspections, improve estimate accuracy, prioritize work, and guide both advisors and technicians in real time.

This project is designed to bridge the gap between technician findings and business execution—ensuring nothing is missed, miscommunicated, or delayed—while enabling scalable, systemized shop operations.

Core Objective

Build a system that ingests DVI/inspection data and outputs:

Clean, structured vehicle assessments
Complete and accurate estimates
Technician-ready workflows
Real-time priority tracking across all vehicles
Advisor coaching and decision support

End goal: a self-managing service workflow system that reduces human error, increases ARO, and enables scalable operations.

DVI / Inspection Standards

“Good” DVI includes:

Clear, specific notes (not vague)
Photos showing actual issue
Supporting evidence (codes, measurements)
Explanation of why it matters
Clear identification of severity

Avoid:

Generic phrases (“needs attention”)
Missing justification
No photos or unclear photos
Estimate / Audit Standards

“Good” estimate includes:

Every DVI finding mapped to a line item
No missing recommended repairs
Clear separation:
Immediate vs future work
Labor + parts fully accounted for

System should flag:

Findings with no estimate line
Partial or unclear repairs
Missing diagnostic steps
Advisor Guidance / Coaching Logic

System should prompt:

“This finding is not on the estimate”
“Vehicle has not been updated in 24 hours”
“Approval received — generate workflow”
“Follow up with customer”
“Technician notes lack justification”

Tone:

Direct, actionable, not verbose
